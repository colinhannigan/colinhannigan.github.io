POLS 347: Latin American Politics

Last Taught: Spring 2018

Why does a landlocked country like Bolivia have a navy? Is the Latin American “new left” becoming old news? What’s the deal with Venezuela? Is Latin America as “ungovernable” as Simón Bolívar claimed? Is the FARC really a political party now? In this course, students will uncover some of the contemporary issues that Latin American countries face: securing peace within and across borders, lessening the gap between the rich and the poor, seeking political inclusion for underrepresented identity groups, rooting out corruption, maintaining stability in the region, and coordinating responses to crises of international concern. In so doing, students will engage with the major debates that inform Latin American political thought, as well as the conflicts, historical memory, policies institutions, and social movements that have brought Latin American politics to this point. Content will alternate between broader theoretical foundations in Latin American and Comparative Politics and illustrative case studies with which to apply and evaluate such theories.
