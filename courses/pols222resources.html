Data Sources

Popular Data Sources
  • Polity Project This link opens in a new window Panel data on democratic indicators on a scale of -10 (consolidated autocracy) to 10 (consolidated autocracy). Examines executive recruitment, constraints on executive authority and political competition.
  • Varieties of Democracy (V-Dem) This link opens in a new window Multidimensional and disaggregated dataset that reflects the complexity of the concept of democracy. The V-Dem project distinguishes between five high-level principles of democracy: electoral, liberal, participatory, deliberative, and egalitarian.
  • Freedom House This link opens in a new window Expert survey ratings and narrative reports on 195 countries and 15 related and disputed territories with indicators of democracy and freedom in the world over time.
  • World Values Survey (WVS) This link opens in a new window International research program examining social, political, economic, religious and cultural values of people in the world through surveys.
  • American National Election Study (NES) This link opens in a new window Academically-run national surveys of voters in the United States, conducted before and after every presidential election.
  • General Social Survey (GSS) This link opens in a new window Nationally representative survey data on contemporary American society intended to allow researchers to monitor and explain trends in opinions, attitudes and behaviors.
  • Armed Conflict Location & Event Data (ACLED) This link opens in a new window Disaggregated data collection, analysis, and crisis mapping project involving information on the dates, actors, locations, fatalities, and types of all reported political violence and protest events around the world.
  • Correlates of War (COW) This link opens in a new window Academic study of the history of warfare aimed at constructing quantitative indicators of key causes of war, international politics, and national capabilities over time.
  • UCDP Armed Conflict Data (ACD) This link opens in a new window The Uppsala Conflict Data Program (UCDP) provides data on organized violence and the oldest ongoing data collection project for civil war, with a history of almost 40 years.
