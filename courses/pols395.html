POLS 395: Model United Nations Seminar

Last Taught: Fall 2022

This course facilitates student preparation for participation in the Harvard National Model United Nations (HNMUN) conference held in February of each academic year. Emphasis is placed on developing studentsâ€™ writing, research, and public speaking skills. In particular, this course will provide students with background knowledge on the various role international organizations play in world politics, with special attention given to the history, structures, and functions of the United Nations (UN). Note, however, that this course is merely a facilitator, as student delegates are expected to be knowledgeable and self-sufficient at the conference, which will require research, writing, and skills development work on their own time. With the support of peers, the instructor, and class discussion, students will develop plausible solutions to contemporary global issues, such as, but not limited to, human rights, protection of the environment, economic development, arms control and proliferation of WMD, and the complexities surrounding international conflict.
