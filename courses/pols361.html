POLS 361: Senior Research Seminar: Public Affairs

Last Taught: Spring 2020

In this course students demonstrate the research, analytical, and writing skills they have developed through the political science major at CSB/SJU by writing a focused 30-35 page research paper and publicly presenting their research. The thesis project proceeds through a number of required stages: identifying a research question, producing an annotated bibliography, writing a review of the scholarly literature relevant to the research question, constructing a research design, producing several drafts of the paper itself with feedback from the instructor and fellow students, orally presenting one's research, and submitting the polished final project at the end of the semester. The POLS 361 course label specifically designates research in Public Affairs, but students are permitted to write/conduct research within any subfield of political science as long as they have an appropriately framed research question, and are willing where necessary to seek input from faculty members with expertise in that field. Research projects focusing on public policy will include the following: a definition of a specific policy problem; a review of the scholarly literature (as well as other relevant sources) related to that problem; analysis and evaluation of alternative policy options for addressing the problem; and a recommendation of a specific course of action. Research projects in other fields of political science do not need to make specific policy recommendations. But they must include a definition of the problem or “puzzle” the paper is addressing, a review of scholarly literature as well as relevant primary sources, examination of competing arguments/explanations, and a focused theoretical argument backed by evidence. In addition to the research paper process, students produce a life-and-career document called a Plan for Professional Development (PPD), and write an epilogue to their Political Autobiographies from earlier political science coursework.
