POLS 342: Democratization

Last Taught: Spring 2022

Does it come through a hole in the air? Or a crack in wall? Democracy has to come from somewhere. Do you often wonder why some countries have long-serving dictators while other countries elect their leaders at regular intervals? Have you been worrying that democracies the world over appear to be slowly eroding? Do you want to be able to win an argument with your roommate who thinks Cuba is democratic because “they have elections, bro”? This course breaks open the black box of democratization. Our aim is to uncover the predictors of regime change and regime stability throughout the world. We’ll examine what it takes to become a democracy, how to keep it that way, and why it sometimes fails. Along the way, students will explore recent trends in regime change (to and from democracy), structural and process-oriented explanations for democratization, regime durability, authoritarian survival strategies, democratic backsliding, the importance of civil society and political culture, democracy promotion as foreign policy, and the international behavior of democracies and autocracies. Whether you’re longing to build democracy or subvert it, this is your chance to stand up and be counted. Democracy happens when people show up; “sail on, sail on, O mighty ship of state!” because “Democracy’s comin’...”
