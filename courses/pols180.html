POLS 180: Politics & Political Life

Last Taught: Fall 2022

Politics can be boiled down to simply “the authoritative allocation of values” within a society. This course is less about the ‘how’ of politics and more about the ‘why’—why exactly do you value what you do? The purpose of this seminar is twofold: first, to expose students to some of the fundamental questions and concepts that drive political inquiry; and second, to implore students to critically examine the sources of their political socialization and reflect the values that define their political experiences. Our discussions will have us wondering: what is human nature? Where do our ideologies come from? Who makes up our community? Who defines it? How do we differ, and in what ways are we fundamentally similar? What can we do to start bridging the divide?
