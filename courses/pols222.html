POLS 222: Analysis: U.S. Policy/Election

Last Taught: Spring 2020

What makes political science a “science?” Smash Mouth famously said, “Somebody once told me the world is gonna roll me. I ain't the sharpest tool in the shed.” Every day, humans make observations about the world around us. Consider how often people rely on instinct, appeals to authority, second-hand knowledge, ideology, or individual experiences to draw incorrect conclusions about human behavior in the wider world. But what if those sources of information are singular, biased, or flukes? How can we be sure that the sample truly represents the population of cases? Even Smash Mouth heard a rumor from an unnamed source and took it as fact. In this class, however, we will learn how to systematically collect, analyze, and interpret evidence using quantitative methods and visualizations; in other words, we learn one of the ways in which political science becomes a “science.” Students will explore interesting research questions, develop logically sound hypotheses for them, and discover methods of testing those hypotheses with empirical data in order to draw more appropriate and generalizable conclusions about human behavior. So, join us for a journey through quantitative empirical analysis. Improve your understanding of data. Hone your research skills. Polish your analytical writing skills. Become the sharpest tool in the shed.
